AI-Based Urban Air Impact Analysis System for Factory and Power Plant Emissions (GAIA Project)

This project aims to analyze and predict the urban air quality impact of industrial and power plant emissions using artificial intelligence.

Based on seven years (2018â€“2024) of time-series data collected from the national Tele-Monitoring System (TMS), the team developed a hybrid prediction model combining the AERMOD physical diffusion model with a Transformer-based deep learning architecture.

A data pipeline automatically collects multi-source datasets from TMS, the Korea Meteorological Administration, and AirKorea APIs. It performs data cleaning, gap filling (Forward Fill, Interpolation, LSTM Imputation), and temporal feature engineering. The PyTorch-based Transformer model learns multivariate time-series relationships among wind, weather, and pollutant levels, while a Gaussian Diffusion algorithm probabilistically models diffusion uncertainty.

The prediction results are visualized in real time through a Streamlit web dashboard, enabling users to interactively explore emission scenarios, 3D plume diffusion maps, and real vs. predicted data comparisons. Visualization modules use Folium, GeoPandas, and Xarray for spatial heatmaps and time-lapse animations.

The GAIA system serves as a data-driven decision support platform for environmental policy planning, industrial strategy analysis, and disaster response simulation. Future work includes integrating IoT sensors and extending the platform to nationwide and climate-change scenarios.

Tech Stack: PyTorch, NumPy, Pandas, Scikit-learn, Folium, GeoPandas, Xarray, Streamlit

Crop Quality Assessment Using Image Processing and Artificial Intelligence (Guardians of Farm Project)

This project developed an AI-based crop quality assessment system designed to detect plant diseases and evaluate leaf health efficiently in smart farming environments.

The initial approach utilized GroundingDINO and SAM (Segment Anything Model) for unsupervised leaf segmentation and RGB-based classification of normal and diseased leaves. However, the system showed limitations under data imbalance, where average RGB values were biased, leading to false classifications.

To overcome this, the team built a large-scale labeled dataset by combining open-source images from Kaggle with real-world collected photos and performed data labeling and augmentation using RoboFlow. The detection framework was then replaced with a YOLOv8-based supervised learning model, significantly improving both accuracy and inference speed.

The final system visualizes detected leaves with colored bounding boxes and quantifies crop health into five levelsâ€”Normal, Caution, Warning, Danger, and Criticalâ€”based on the ratio of healthy to diseased leaves. The trained YOLOv8 model achieved a mean mAP@0.5 of 0.81 and AP of 0.846 for diseased leaves, validating its robustness and applicability.

This project demonstrates a successful transition from unsupervised visual grounding models (GroundingDINO + SAM) to a high-performance YOLOv8-based supervised framework, and proposes its potential integration into IoT-driven smart farming systems for real-time crop quality monitoring.

Tech Stack: Python, PyTorch, YOLOv8, GroundingDINO, SAM, RoboFlow, OpenCV, NumPy
